# Giulio Turrisi

<p align="justify">
<img src="gturrisi_pic.jpg" width="200" align="right" hspace="12" vspace="8"/>
He is a postdoctoral researcher at the [Dynamic Legged Systems](https://dls.iit.it/) lab at the Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia, where he focuses on machine learning and optimal control methods for robot locomotion and manipulation. He received my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Pisa in 2016, followed by a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics from Sapienza University of Rome in 2018. He then pursued a PhD in Automatic Control, Bioengineering, and Operations Research at the same institution, where he was part of the [DIAG Robotics Lab](https://www.diag.uniroma1.it/~labrob/), completing my doctorate in 2022. His research interests lie at the intersection of learning-based control and robotics, with a particular emphasis on developing robust and efficient methods for complex, real-world robotic systems. he has also contributed to the academic community as an associate editor for major conferences such as IEEE Humanoids and IROS.

Beyond research, he is passionate about open-source software, reproducible research, and teaching.
